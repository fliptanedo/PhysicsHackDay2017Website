UCR Physics Hack Day 2017

7:00 pm Friday, May 12, 2017 - 5:30 pm Saturday, May 13th, 2017; UCR Physics Building

Bridging Programming and Scientists

UCR Physics Hack Day is a 24-hour coding-fest. Participants come together to play with scientific datasets. Bring your clever, fun, and creative sides to breathe life into data from physics and astronomy! In subsequent years we hope to expand Physics Hack Day to other schools in Riverside and the broader Southern California community.

Hackathons are challenges to rapidly prototype hardware and software. UCR Physics Hack Day highlights the playful and creative side of scientific data. Successful projects visualize/sonify science in novel ways and encourages curiosity and further open-source exploration.

Our goal is for UCR Physics Hack Day to become the permiere science hackathon in Southern California and to join the Science Hack Day network. In subseqent events, we expect to host students from other colleges and reach out to the local Riverside STEM community.

Rules and Guidelines

Borrowing from the IgNobel Prize, our goal is to let our science make people laugh, then make them think. Teams may have up to 4 students. All code must be posted to GitHub.

Criteria

Teams will be evaluated on:

  • Use of physics and astronomy data. Suggested datasets will be released just before the event, but any publicly available data may be used.
  • Creativity. Show us something new!
  • Potential for development. Is this something that can be extended and used for further science outreach? Can other physics hackers modify it?
  • Artistry. Visualizations, sonifications, etc.
  • Completeness. Show us a working prototype.
