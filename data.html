Datasets

You are not required to use any of the following datasets, but these might be useful starting points if you're looking for an idea!

  • Particle Physics Playground: GitHub respository of Particle Physics data samples and tools to analyze them. Sample Jupyter notebooks show how to access data. (Thanks to Prof. Matth Bellis of Sienna College!)
  • Hipparcos star dataset. Positions and distances of stars observed by the Hipparcos satellite.
  • Fermi-LAT Point Source Catalog. Catalog of gamma ray sources in the sky observed by the Fermi Large Area Telescope.
  • ASAIP Datasets. Astronomical dataset collection from the Penn State Astrostatistics and Astroinformatics Portal.
  • Astronomical Images Over the Web, Harvard Astronomy collection
  • Astronomy Datasets on data.gov.
  • Earthquak visualization/sonification. Matt Bellis' project pitch to Science Hack Day: SF 2014.

Examples

A few open source examples that you can modify and hack to develop your own Physics Hack Day projects. Be sure to credit the original authors and link to their code in your README.md files!

  • Particle Clicker: Particle Clicker is a game that was made during the CERN Webfest 2014. The idea is borrowed from Cookie Clicker, an amazing and addictive cookie-themed game.
  • Particle Wind Chime, one of Matt Bellis' Science Hack Day Project
  • Conway's Game of Life in Python
  • Examples from Science Hack Day 2015. Check out: CO2 Chrome Extension, Solar Archive.
